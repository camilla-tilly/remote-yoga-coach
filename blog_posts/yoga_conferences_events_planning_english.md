# Yoga at Conferences — Planning Guide for Event Organizers

You're planning a conference or big event. You want people to be focused, energized, and present.

You're thinking about adding yoga. But when? How long? Will people stay or hide in their hotel rooms?

Here's a guide for planning yoga in your event schedule.

## Why yoga at conferences works

Conferences are draining. People sit long, listen a lot, think a lot. Energy drops through the day.

A yoga session breaks this in a unique way:
- People get to move their bodies without it being competitive
- There's a shared experience
- Energy rises after the session (or calms down, depending on what you need)

Plus, people are more receptive to the rest of the program after yoga. They're more present, less exhausted.

## Where in the schedule should yoga go?

**First thing in the morning (before sessions start):**
Best for: Kicking off the day with energy
Length: 20-30 minutes
Effect: People are alert, focused for the day

**At lunch (before or after food):**
Best for: Mental break from sessions
Length: 15-20 minutes
Effect: People wake up after lunch slump

**Afternoon (at 3-4 PM energy slump):**
Best for: Giving a second wind
Length: 15-20 minutes
Effect: People are energized for the final part of the day

**Toward end of day:**
Best for: Calming people before evening program or social
Length: 20-30 minutes
Effect: People are calm and present

## How long should the yoga session be?

**5-10 minutes:** Micro-yoga. Quick breathing exercise and stretching. Fits between sessions.

**15-20 minutes:** Perfect for a lunch-break yoga. People have time to relax without taking too much time.

**30-45 minutes:** Longer session. Real time for people to relax and build shared experience.

Most conferences run 20-30 minutes.

## FAQ

**Do people need yoga mats?**
No. They can stand in whatever clothes they have and do yoga.

**What if people don't want to go?**
That's okay. Make it completely voluntary. People who don't go sit on the hotel couch instead.

**What type of yoga is best for conferences?**
Calm, focused yoga. No power yoga. People need to be able to do it in office clothes.

**How much space do we need?**
A conference room or outdoor space where people can stand. Not much more than that.

**Can we do it online for participants on Teams?**
Yes, absolutely. But it's often better in person for conference attendees.
