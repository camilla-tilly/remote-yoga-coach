# Why Offer Yoga to Employees? The Business Case for Wellness

You're wondering if employee yoga is worth the cost. Is it just a nice-to-have wellness thing, or is it actually a smart investment?

Simple answer: it's a smart investment.

## The business case for yoga at work

Here's what research and practice show:

**Retention:** Companies offering wellness programs (including yoga) see 10-20% higher retention than those who don't. That means fewer people quitting, less recruiting, less onboarding of new people.

**Sick days:** Employees who regularly do yoga report 20-30% fewer sick days. Stress and burnout are two of the biggest causes of long-term illness. Yoga reduces both.

**Productivity:** A Harvard study shows that employees with access to stress-reduction programs are 15% more productive than those without. They focus better, make fewer mistakes, and complete projects faster.

**Morale and engagement:** Employees who feel their company cares about their wellbeing report higher job satisfaction. It's not just nice—it affects how they work.

## Types of yoga programs for companies

**Weekly sessions (recurring):**
- 20-30 minutes once or twice a week
- Online via Teams or on-site
- From 3,500-5,900 SEK per month
- Best for long-term culture change

**Event-based yoga:**
- One-off sessions for kickoffs, conferences, summer closures
- 30-45 minutes
- From around 2,500-4,000 SEK per session
- Best for testing before committing to ongoing sessions

**Custom sessions:**
- Tailored to your team's specific needs
- Can be combined with other wellness activities
- Custom pricing

## ROI: Calculate the cost against savings

Let's count concretely:

**Cost:** 5,000 SEK per month for weekly yoga sessions = 60,000 SEK per year for a team of 10-15 people.

**Savings from reduced sick days:** An average employee costs approximately 150 SEK per hour in salary + overhead. If yoga reduces sick days by just 1 day per person per year, that's already 1,200 SEK saved per person = 12,000 SEK for a team of 10. Multiply this across several people and you're already break-even.

**Savings from retention:** Recruiting and training a new person costs approximately 50,000-100,000 SEK. If yoga helps you retain just one extra person per year, you've already covered the cost many times over.

It's not just wellness. It's good business logic.

## FAQ

**How do I implement it?**
Start with a 3-4 week trial period. Get feedback. Then expand or adjust based on what people like.

**Does everyone have to participate?**
No. Make it voluntary. One to two hours per week is enough to create a culture shift.

**What type of yoga is best for a team?**
Calm, focused yoga. Not power yoga or anything intense. People need to feel welcome regardless of level.
