# How Often Should Companies Offer Yoga? Frequency, Format & Results

You're thinking about introducing yoga at your workplace. But how often does it need to happen for it to actually work?

Once a month? Once a week? Every day?

The answer: it depends on what results you want. But here's what research says.

## Habit formation and yoga

It takes about 66 days to build a new habit, according to research. That's two months.

Yoga is the same. Once a week for two months, and people actually start wanting yoga. It becomes a habit.

Once a month? It takes much longer and people forget between sessions.

## Weekly sessions (the best frequency)

**Frequency:** Once to twice per week
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes per session
**Results visible:** 4-6 weeks

With weekly sessions you see:
- Reduced stress on a daily basis
- Better focus and productivity
- People actively expecting yoga every week (it becomes a habit)
- Better retention and morale

**Format:**
- Online via Teams for maximum participation (easy to join)
- On-site for maximum team-building
- A mix of both is perfect

## Monthly events (better than nothing, but not optimal)

**Frequency:** Once per month
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Results:** Takes much longer to see

With monthly sessions you get:
- A chance for people to try yoga
- Much stronger focus on event than habit
- Longer timeline before people see results (3-4 months)
- Lower retention (people forget between sessions)

It's better than nothing. But weekly is significantly better.

## Habit formation timeline

**Week 1-2:** Novelty excitement. People are interested because it's new.

**Week 3-4:** Interest drops slightly. People understand what yoga is. Some stop coming.

**Week 5-6:** Those who stay start noticing effects. They notice stress relief, better focus. They actually start expecting yoga.

**Week 8-12:** Yoga is an established habit. New people might start because they've seen results in others.

## Remote (Teams) vs. on-site

**Remote via Teams:**
- Higher participation (people don't need to move)
- Easier to schedule
- People can join from their desk at lunch
- Less team-building

**On-site:**
- Lower participation (people have to move)
- Greater team-building effect
- More impactful
- Harder to schedule

**Best? Mix of both.** One week on Teams, one week on-site. Or twice a week on Teams, once a month on-site for bigger events.

## FAQ

**Can we start with once a month and then build up?**
Yes, it works. But it takes much longer to see results.

**What's the minimum frequency to see results?**
Twice per month. But once a week is much better.

**Can we do it just in summer or winter months?**
You can, but it'll be harder to maintain momentum. Consistency is key.

**How long do we need it for people to actually want to continue?**
After 8-12 weeks of regular sessions it's often established enough that people actually want to continue.

**What does it cost?**
Weekly online sessions cost around 3,500-5,900 SEK per month depending on team size.
