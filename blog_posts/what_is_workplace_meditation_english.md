# What Is Workplace Meditation? Guided Breathing for Focus & Calm

Meditation is a word that can make people nervous. They think of monks in caves or yogis on mountaintops. But workplace meditation is something completely different—and much more practical.

It's two minutes with your eyes closed before a meeting. It's a guided breathing exercise when stress rises. It's a chance to pause your brain in the middle of your workday.

## The difference between yoga and meditation

Many people think yoga and meditation are the same thing. They're not.

Yoga is movement—stretching, breathing, and moving your body through space. It's active.

Meditation is rest—focus, breathing, and mental concentration on one thing. It's calm.

Workplace meditation is short, guided, and focused. Not transcendental meditation where you sit for hours. It's breathing exercises, guided visualizations, and body scans that take 5–15 minutes.

## How does it work?

A typical session might look like this:

- You sit comfortably (on a chair, mat, or wherever you want)
- An instructor guides you through a breathing exercise or visualization
- You focus on your breath, or a mantra, or the sensation of being here
- The session ends peacefully

It's not magic. It's neuroscience. Your nervous system has two modes: stress (sympathetic) and rest (parasympathetic). Meditation switches you from stress to rest.

## Why do workplaces need meditation?

Workplaces are stressful. Emails, meetings, deadlines, and expectations create a constant buzz of activity. Your brain never fully turns off. Your body never fully relaxes.

Meditation breaks this cycle. Even two minutes of guided breathing can lower your heart rate, decrease cortisol levels, and give your brain a rest.

Research shows that employees who practice meditation:
- Are more focused and productive
- Have less anxiety and depression
- Sleep better
- Are less likely to leave their job

## What's the difference between meditation and corporate yoga?

Both yoga and meditation are common in many workplaces. Here's the difference:

**Yoga** = movement + breathing (20–30 min, often physical)
**Meditation** = stillness + focus (5–15 min, purely mental)

Many companies offer both. Once a week you might have yoga on Friday morning. Once a week you might have meditation on Monday morning before meetings start. They complement each other.

## Who can meditate?

Everyone. You don't need to be "good at meditation." There's no such thing as failing at meditation.

Meditation is not about thinking of nothing. It's about noticing when your mind wanders and gently bringing it back. It's training for your attention—nothing more.

Not religious either. Secular meditation focuses on breathing and presence—no faith required.

## FAQ

**How long is a meditation session?**
From 2 minutes (quick pause) to 20 minutes (deeper practice). Most companies start with 10–15 minutes.

**Can we do it on Teams?**
Yes, absolutely. Meditation actually works better online than yoga—you can sit in your own chair at home, and nobody needs to see your face.

**Do we need to close the doors?**
It helps, but it's not necessary. Many companies do meditation in a quiet conference room or simply at their desks.

**What does it cost?**
Meditation is often part of a larger wellness package with yoga, or can be booked separately. About the same as yoga—from around 3,500 SEK per month.

**Does it actually work?**
Yes. The research is clear: meditation reduces stress, improves focus, and improves mental health. You just need consistency—a few weeks of regular sessions before you notice the difference.
