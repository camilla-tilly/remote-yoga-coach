# Yoga for Screen Fatigue — Relieving Digital Burnout at Work

You're staring at a screen 6, 7, or 8 hours a day. It wasn't so long ago that was just office work. Now it's office work plus all the emails at home, plus scrolling on your phone on breaks.

Your neck hurts. Your eyes are tired. Your brain feels foggy.

It's called "digital fatigue" and it's real. And yoga can actually help.

## What is digital fatigue, really?

Digital fatigue is not just tiredness. It's a combination of:

- **Eye strain:** Screen light, focusing at close range all day creates strain
- **Neck tension:** We lean forward toward screens without noticing
- **Mental fog:** Your brain gets tired from constant stimulation and decision-making
- **Sleep problems:** Blue light from screens disrupts melatonin production

Together, it leaves many people completely drained by day's end.

## How yoga helps with digital fatigue

**For the eyes:**
Yoga often includes focus on distance and eye movement. It opposes hours of screen focus. Simple things like looking out a window or slowly moving eyes in different directions can completely change how you feel.

**For the neck:**
Almost all yoga includes neck stretching and shoulder opening. It opposes the forward-leaned position we're in at screens.

**For the brain:**
Yoga is the opposite of digital stimulation. It's stillness, focused, slow movement. Your brain completely relaxes.

**For sleep:**
Yoga reduces blue light exposure and stimulation, which helps melatonin rise naturally.

## Practical solutions: yoga at work

**Micro-breaks with yoga:**
Every hour, take 2 minutes for neck stretching, eye exercises, and shoulder rolls. Takes almost no time but makes enormous difference.

**Lunch yoga:**
A 20-minute session at lunch focused on neck, shoulders, and eyes. Complete game-changer for how you feel the rest of the day.

**Weekly sessions:**
A longer session (30-45 minutes) once or twice a week for deeper reset.

## FAQ

**Can we do yoga at our desks?**
Yes! Many exercises can be done sitting on a chair at your desk without even standing up.

**How long before I notice a difference?**
Immediate with micro-breaks. One to two weeks of regular sessions for bigger change.

**What does it cost to introduce yoga for digital fatigue?**
Same as regular corporate yoga. From around 3,500-5,900 SEK per month for weekly sessions.

**Can we do it on Teams?**
Yes, absolutely. You're already at the screen, you can do yoga at your desk at home or the office.

**What type of yoga is best for this?**
Yoga focused on neck, shoulders, and eyes. Not power yoga or anything intense.
